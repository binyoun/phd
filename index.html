Research Gap

Human-computer interaction has largely developed within Western technological paradigms — efficiency, optimization, human-centered control. Eastern cosmological thinking, organized around circulation, qi, elemental correspondence, and relational presence, offers a fundamentally different model for how bodies, environments, and technologies meet. Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics argues that technology is never culturally neutral: Eastern technics carry their own cosmological logic, distinct from Western instrumentalism. Yet limited research has translated these frameworks into participatory media systems or installation practice. This research addresses that gap through making.

Theoretical Framework
Cosmotechnics
Yuk Hui

Technology is cosmologically situated — never neutral. Eastern technics are organised around qi, circulation, and elemental correspondence rather than efficiency and control. 오행 (Five Elements) is not metaphor: a functional technical system with its own diagnostic and responsive logic.

Feminist STS
Haraway · Suchman · Puig de la Bellacasa

Technology as value-embedded, relational practice. Situated knowledge over claimed objectivity. Care, interdependence, and maintenance as design values rather than efficiency and optimization. Interaction as a distributed network of relations, not a discrete interface.

Posthumanism /
New Materialism
Barad · Braidotti

Human and nonhuman agency as distributed and entangled. Environment as participant, not backdrop. Matter has agency. Interaction understood as ecological entanglement rather than subject-object exchange.

Gear One · SCD Seed Funded 2026
Circulation: A Triadic Interactive System Inspired by Cupping Therapy

Three handheld sensing devices inspired by cupping therapy (부항 / giác hơi). Each maps participant pulse and pressure to Five Elements (오행) colour logic — not Western RGB. Collective synchronisation shifts the room's ambient field. Cosmotechnics as interaction design. Presented VFCD September 2026.

Further installations to be developed during candidature.

Researcher
수지침
Hand Acupuncture · 3 years

East Asian medical practice studied for three years from age thirteen. The diagnostic logic of 수지침 — reading the body through relational pressure points, not isolated data — is the direct source of the sensing logic in Circulation. The cosmotechnics claim is learned in the hands, not in books.

사물놀이
Korean Traditional Percussion · 4 instruments · 2 years · Foreign Language High School

Two years playing 사물놀이 — 꽹과리 (thunder), 징 (wind), 장구 (rain), 북 (cloud). The four instruments map to cosmic elements; ensemble performance is collective cosmological ordering through rhythm. 사물놀이 is a cosmotechnical practice. The sensing and synchronisation logic of Circulation — three bodies finding a shared pulse — has its earliest form here.

명리학 / 유교사상
Four Pillars Cosmology · Confucian Thought

Formal study of 명리학 (Four Pillars of Destiny) and 유교사상 (Confucian thought), including the Neo-Confucian debate between 퇴계 (Toegye) and 율곡 (Yulgok) on 사단칠정. The Five Elements (오행) as a living cosmological system — not a borrowed aesthetic. This is prior formation, not theoretical framework.

성균관대학교
Sungkyunkwan University · est. 1398 · BA in Family and Consumer Sciences (Marketing)

BA at Korea's oldest university — founded 1398 as the national Confucian academy of the Joseon Dynasty. Degree in Family and Consumer Sciences with a marketing focus: not a humanities programme, but an institution whose entire architecture, ritual calendar, and institutional identity are Confucian. The formation is environmental. 유교사상 and 명리학 were absorbed through living inside that context and through personal study — not through a curriculum.

Research Fit

Dr. Nancy Mauro-Flude's work on decolonising digital culturescapes, permacomputing, and feminist-led technological governance shares the fundamental premise of this research: that technology is not culturally neutral, and that alternative technological paradigms are both possible and necessary.

Her current supervision of AI disalienation through creative practice, and her founding of espX — Ecofeminist Studio for Permacomputing — align directly with research grounded in non-Western technics, collective care, and low-tech sensing systems. Her 2025 publication Thinking with Shells: Decolonising Digital Culturescapes sits in direct dialogue with the cosmotechnical framing of this project. This research belongs at the intersection of those commitments.
