Who

Four cities — not a biography, but a comparative research position built through living. Eastern formation (Seoul), European encounter (Paris), American MFA (California, CSULB 2023), Southeast Asian research environment (Ho Chi Minh City). The transnational Asian perspective this research requires is not acquired — it is the structure of a life. HCMC as situated research condition: urban density, hybrid physical-digital life, and a regional cosmotechnical history that includes 오행 through Vietnamese giác hơi.

Hand Acupuncture (수지침) — 3 years. East Asian medical practice whose diagnostic logic reads the body through relational pressure points, not isolated data. Direct source of the sensing logic in Circulation.

Korean Traditional Percussion (사물놀이) — 4 instruments (Thunder · Wind · Rain · Cloud), 2 years, Foreign Language High School. Ensemble performance as collective cosmological ordering through rhythm. The synchronisation logic of Circulation — three bodies finding a shared pulse — has its earliest form here.

Four Pillars Cosmology (명리학) + Confucian Thought (유교사상) — including the Toegye (퇴계) vs Yulgok (율곡) debate on the Four Beginnings and Seven Emotions (사단칠정). Five Elements (오행) as a living cosmological system — not a borrowed aesthetic.

Sungkyunkwan University (성균관대학교), est. 1398 — Korea's national Confucian academy of the Joseon Dynasty. BA in Consumer and Family Sciences + Business (Marketing). Not a humanities programme: a Confucian environment absorbed through institutional formation, ritual calendar, and personal study — not curriculum.

Photography — started getting serious in Paris. Installation and sculptural forms — CSULB MFA 2023 (ㅁotherisland, Breathscape, Somatic Blinds, YOUR LEAVES TOUCH MY PLANES, Projective Verse of ㅇ). AR & XR — from 2025, after attending ISEA 2025 in Seoul. Computational media — ongoing. Medium changes; the inquiry — relational perception, cosmological ordering, collective sensing — continues.

Research Gap

Interaction design has developed within models of individual control: a user acts, a system responds. Eastern cosmological thinking organises interaction differently — around relational systems where bodies, environments, and technologies are in circulation with one another, not hierarchically arranged. The question is not how an individual uses a system, but how a system can hold and respond to the relations between bodies.

Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics argues that technology is never culturally neutral. Eastern technics carry a distinct cosmological logic — organised around elemental correspondence and relational order rather than Western instrumentalism. This is not philosophical decoration: it is a different grammar for how technical systems are designed. Circulation — the movement between body and environment, between bodies in shared space — is the operative concept.

Within this grammar, how bodies sense and respond to each other is understood as a care-informed process: wellbeing, collective state, social and emotional attunement. Limited research has translated these cosmotechnical frameworks into cultural interaction models. This research addresses that gap through making.

Research Questions

Interaction design has been built within models of individual control: a user acts, a system responds. Eastern cosmological thinking organises interaction around relational systems — where bodies, environments, and technologies are in circulation with one another. This cosmotechnical grammar, organised around care, collective sensing, and relational order, has not been translated into cultural interaction models.

What

  • How can relational sensing systems embody cosmological logic between human and non-human agents?
  • How can participatory cultural interaction models attend to collective wellbeing and emotional attunement?
  • How can non-Western cosmological perspectives offer an alternative grammar to human-centered interaction design?

Theoretical Framework

Technology is cosmologically situated — never neutral. Eastern technics carry a distinct cosmological logic organised around elemental correspondence and relational order rather than Western instrumentalism. 오행 (Five Elements) is not metaphor: a functional technical system with its own diagnostic and responsive logic. Cosmotechnics provides the foundational claim: that the design grammar of a technical system carries the cosmological order of the culture that produced it.

Feminist STS frames technology as value-embedded and relational — care, interdependence, and maintenance as design values rather than efficiency. Actor-Network Theory extends this: non-human entities are actors, not objects. The cupping cup is a participant. The Five Elements system is a network. Sensing devices are not tools but co-agents in a distributed interaction. Together they provide the ethical orientation and the structural grammar for understanding how cultural interaction models distribute agency across human and non-human participants.

Kuan-Hsing Chen's Asia as Method argues that Asia Pacific can function as a comparative lens to decentre Euro-American knowledge production — not by substituting one centre for another, but by building relational frameworks within the region. Appadurai's account of cultural flows maps how practices, objects, and ideas move across contexts and transform in transit. For this research, the Korea-Vietnam corridor — cupping therapy shared across cultures, the M-Node cross-cultural exchange, Sacred Tree's cross-cultural encounter — is not biographical context. It is a research method. Cultural interaction models emerge from and are tested within these intercultural flows.

The body is a site of knowledge production. Practices learned in the hands — three years of hand acupuncture (수지침), two years of 사물놀이, the diagnostic hold of cupping therapy — constitute a form of understanding that cannot be accessed through text alone. Merleau-Ponty's phenomenology of embodiment establishes perception as fundamentally bodily. Shusterman's somaesthetics frames the body as an instrument of knowledge. Sarah Pink's sensory ethnography provides the methodological bridge: cultural fieldwork conducted through bodily attention, not detached observation. This framework legitimises the research formation as epistemological, not anecdotal.

Methodology
  1. Practice-based research — artistic production as primary mode of inquiry
  2. Research through design — iterative prototyping as knowledge generation
  3. Installation prototyping — interactive environments as experimental platforms
  4. Sensor-based system development — physiological and environmental sensing
  5. Exhibition testing and audience observation — live interaction as data
  6. Sensory ethnography (Sarah Pink) — cultural fieldwork conducted through bodily and sensory attention
  7. Auto-ethnography — the researcher's transnational formation and embodied practices as primary research data
Practice Trajectory
Øryn / 륜
Research Universe

An ongoing practice-based research project — an AI-assisted post-human presence grounded in East Asian cosmology and speculative ecology. Not a character: a relational archive being that exists across generative image, XR, voice, and installation. Its body dissolves at the edges into mist. Its mount, Maek (맥), is not an animal but a cosmic energy organism. Together they inhabit Gye (계) — an archive dimension where cultural memory and ecological presence meet. Each work in the PhD is a phase within Øryn's ecology. A planned research paper frames the project within questions of digital immortality and the artist-created entity as both artwork and research methodology.

First Physical Manifestation

Three handheld sensing devices inspired by cupping therapy — 부항 (Korean) / giác hơi (Vietnamese). Each reads participant pulse and pressure and maps to Five Elements (오행) colour logic — not Western RGB. As participants synchronise, pulse rhythms converging and pressure aligning, the colours of the three cups begin to harmonise. Cups are the seed; the room, when present, is the bloom. Cosmotechnics as interaction design. To be presented at VFCD September 2026.

Further installations to be developed during candidature.

Research Fit

Dr. Nancy Mauro-Flude's work on decolonising digital culturescapes, permacomputing, and feminist-led technological governance shares the fundamental premise of this research: that technology is not culturally neutral, and that alternative technological paradigms are both possible and necessary.

Her current supervision of AI disalienation through creative practice, and her founding of espX — Ecofeminist Studio for Permacomputing — align directly with research grounded in non-Western technics, collective care, and low-tech sensing systems. Her 2025 publication Thinking with Shells: Decolonising Digital Culturescapes sits in direct dialogue with the cosmotechnical framing of this project. This research belongs at the intersection of those commitments.
