Research Questions
Why

Human-computer interaction has been shaped by Western technological paradigms — efficiency, optimization, human-centered control. Eastern cosmological systems, organized around circulation, qi, elemental correspondence, and relational presence, embed a fundamentally different understanding of how bodies, environments, and technologies meet. This alternative logic has not been translated into participatory media or installation practice.

What

How can Eastern cosmotechnics inform alternative paradigms for interactive media systems?

How
  • How can environmental sensing systems embody cosmological relationality between human and non-human agents?
  • How can participatory installations function as experimental interaction environments?
  • How might non-Western cosmological perspectives challenge dominant human-centered interaction models?
Contribution
Theoretical Extending cosmotechnics into interaction design discourse — establishing non-Western technics as a legitimate design paradigm.
Methodological A practice-based framework integrating media art, cosmotechnical philosophy, and feminist STS as a model for future research.
Practical New models for participatory interactive environments that use sensing technologies to enact relational, care-based interaction.
Research Gap

Human-computer interaction has largely developed within Western technological paradigms emphasizing usability, efficiency, and optimization. Eastern cosmological thinking — organized around circulation, qi, elemental correspondence, and relational presence — offers a fundamentally different model for how bodies, environments, and technologies might meet. Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics argues that technology is never culturally neutral: Eastern technics carry their own cosmological logic, distinct from Western instrumentalism. Yet limited research has translated these frameworks into participatory media systems or installation practice. This research addresses that gap through making.

Key Works
The Universe
Øryn / 륙

An AI-assisted media entity and relational archive being — built from East Asian cosmology and speculative ecology. Distributed across generative image, XR, voice, and installation. Not a character: a post-human presence and research vehicle. The universe within which the PhD practice operates.

Gear One · SCD Seed Funded 2026
Circulation: A Triadic Interactive System Inspired by Cupping Therapy

Three handheld sensing devices inspired by cupping therapy (부항 / giác hơi). Each maps participant pulse and pressure to Five Elements (오행) colour logic — not Western RGB. Collective synchronisation shifts the room's ambient field. Cosmotechnics as interaction design. Presented VFCD September 2026.

Further installations to be developed during candidature.

Theoretical Framework
Cosmotechnics
Yuk Hui

Technology is cosmologically situated — never neutral. Eastern technics are organised around qi, circulation, and elemental correspondence rather than efficiency and control. 오행 (Five Elements) is not metaphor: it is a functional technical system with its own diagnostic and responsive logic.

Feminist STS
Haraway · Suchman · Puig de la Bellacasa

Technology as value-embedded, relational practice. Situated knowledge over claimed objectivity. Care, interdependence, and maintenance as design values rather than efficiency and optimization. Interaction as a distributed network of relations, not a discrete interface.

Posthumanism /
New Materialism
Barad · Braidotti

Human and nonhuman agency as distributed and entangled. Environment as participant, not backdrop. Matter has agency. Interaction understood as ecological entanglement rather than subject-object exchange.

Research Fit

Nancy Mauro-Flude's work on decolonising digital culturescapes, permacomputing, and feminist-led technological governance shares the fundamental premise of this research: that technology is not culturally neutral, and that alternative technological paradigms are both possible and necessary.

Her current supervision of AI disalienation through creative practice, and her founding of espX — Ecofeminist Studio for Permacomputing — align directly with research grounded in non-Western technics, collective care, and low-tech sensing systems. This project sits at the intersection of those commitments.
